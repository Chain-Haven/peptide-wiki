# PeptideWiki — SEO Content Roadmap

This is the content plan that turns the technical SEO foundation into rankings and
traffic. Priorities are grounded in live SERP analysis (June 2026): for the
highest-value peptide queries, the first page is dominated by **modest-authority
medspa and vendor blogs** — which a focused, well-cited, tool-backed reference
site can outrank.

## How we win (the thesis)

1. **We already have the tools competitors rank with** — a reconstitution/dosage
   calculator and a side-by-side comparison. Wrapping those in keyword-targeted
   guide content is the fastest path to ranking.
2. **Topical authority** — a dense web of guides that all interlink with our 60+
   peptide pages signals expertise to Google for the whole "peptides" topic.
3. **Commercial intent → affiliate revenue** — "best peptides for X" listicles
   capture buyers and funnel them to our peptide pages and vendor links.

## Cadence & measurement
- Target 2–4 new guides/week; refresh top performers quarterly.
- Every guide interlinks to ≥3 peptide pages + a tool (calculator/compare) + 1–2 sibling guides.
- After publishing, track in Google Search Console (impressions → position → CTR);
  double down on pages reaching positions 5–15 ("striking distance").

## Off-page (not code — owner action)
- Digital PR / outreach for backlinks (the biggest external ranking lever).
- Reddit (r/Peptides), forums, and community presence linking back to guides/tools.
- A named author/medical reviewer byline to strengthen YMYL trust signals.
